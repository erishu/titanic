Given the data on the passengers of the Titanic, I was curious as to which characteristics were more common to those who survived.¶

I looked at 5 different characteristics and their corresponding survival rates:¶

  • Where the passengers departed from
  • What class did they travel in
  • Their sex
  • Whether or not they had siblings or a spouse traveling with them
  • Whether they were older or younger than the mean age of the passengers

In conclusion, a higher percentage of passengers who departed from Cherbourg survived, as well as a higher percentage of passengers traveling in first class, a much higher percentage of female passengers, and a higher percentage of passengers with a spouse or sibling aboard. Interestingly, about the same percentage of passengers survived that were above the mean age as there were who survived that were below the mean age. (These correlations do not imply causation.)¶
